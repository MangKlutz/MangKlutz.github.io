About Us

Nintendo and Niantic teamed up to create Pokémon Go, a wildly popular augmented reality (AR) mobile game that launched in 2016. Niantic developed and published the game, but it wouldn't have been possible without Nintendo and The Pokémon Company, who own the rights to Pokémon. The game uses real-world locations and AR to let players catch, train, and battle virtual Pokémon. This partnership brought Pokémon to a whole new audience and helped to popularize AR gaming.
